# 🚀 Day 29 - SmartCart Customer Segmentation System (Part 2)

## 📌 Overview

Today, I completed the **SmartCart Customer Segmentation System** by applying clustering techniques to identify meaningful customer groups based on spending behavior, engagement levels, and purchasing patterns.

The goal was to transform customer data into actionable insights that can help SmartCart improve customer retention, personalized marketing, and overall business performance.

---

## 🎯 Objective

To group customers with similar behavior into distinct segments and uncover hidden patterns that can support data-driven business decisions.

---

## 📊 Clustering Approach

### K-Means Clustering

- Applied K-Means Clustering on the preprocessed dataset
- Grouped customers based on spending, purchasing frequency, and engagement behavior
- Compared different values of K to find meaningful customer segments

### Elbow Method

- Calculated WCSS (Within Cluster Sum of Squares)
- Identified the optimal number of clusters
- Analyzed cluster compactness and separation

### Silhouette Score

- Evaluated clustering quality
- Measured how well customers fit within their assigned clusters
- Validated cluster effectiveness

## 📈 Customer Segments Identified

### 🟢 High-Value Customers

- High spending behavior
- Frequent purchases
- Strong engagement
- Loyal customer base

### 🟡 Regular Customers

- Moderate spending
- Consistent purchasing activity
- Stable engagement levels

### 🔴 At-Risk Customers

- Low engagement
- High recency values
- Reduced purchasing activity
- Potential churn risk
